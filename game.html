Project Name: In the City

History

This is a unique writeup in the sense that the game itself is just a story, something I'm constructing in a data format digestible by the game engine, which is the actual coding project component of this. In that sense, the two are very tightly coupled and the writeup on the engine can be found here.

I'll offer a brief overview of the story, but I don't want to go into exhaustive detail because there is quite a bit of content. Players begin in the northeast, around St. Jeromes (where I went to elementary school. Most of the game occurs across locations familiar to me from my life). They progress through regions in the following order:

  1. St Jeromes (my elementary school)
  2. Arch Street/lower Chinatown (a cool place to be, and an easy way to introduce the bartering system given the already transaction oriented nature of this area).
  3. Cobbs Creek (the 'safe zone' associated with the Cobbs Creek level is in a bookstore I like which is in West Philly, but not at all Cobbs Creek or even anywhere near it. But calling the location Cobbs Creek allows me to use the creek artistically in light of a drought condition impacting this place).
  4. Art Museum (similar to the cobbs creek thing, the location was chosen mostly because it is a good place to introduce gameplay elements which incorporate the player's perception. The art museum becomes a House of Leaves esque labarynth).
  5. FDR Park (there's not a lot to this. I was writing the story in May, and had ridden my bike down that way the day I was thinking about locations. It was a nice day and put me in a good mood, so I decided to throw the park in as a level).
  6. Temple (where I am currently attending school, and around where I have historically lived).
  7. Port Richmond (just a cool place to hang out. The gameplay here is more whimsical than in other regions, particularly the mentally harrowing Temple level).
  8. Manayunk (in the far future world we see the hills have haphazardly collapsed into the Schuylkill River, creating an alien landscape of cliffs and crags. The fact that the river is not nearly deep or wide enough to accommodate this notwithstanding).
  9. City Hall (this is where the Annihilation influence comes in. I try to invoke the spire of city hall like the towerpit/lighthouse for a surreal ending sequence)

Beyond that, it's a survival adventure game in a far future, loosely dystopic world with some D&D influence. It's meant to build towards a kind of downer ending, with a number of themes which will become clear in that ending. Further updates to this page will occur regularly (albeit infrequently) as I build this into actual game data.

Prior to 1/30/25

Created in JSON the first half of the first region. Progress was incredibly slow-going and significantly repetitive, so I broke from proper region creation in favor of laying out templates which I can use for rooms and adjacencies throughout all levels to reduce the repetitive nature of this work. To that end, I created a few template houses which can be modified to fit any level, as well as a few different sorts of industrial buildings. The idea is that, for example, the school might be the industrial building on its own. The art museum is just 3 of these industrial buildings put together in a sort of 'U' shape.
